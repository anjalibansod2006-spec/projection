Expectorants

  • Drugs that help remove sputum from respiratory tract either by increasing fluidity or volume expelled by coughing.

Expectorants (Continued)

  • Cough is productive reflex to expel irritants from respiratory tract.
  • May be productive or non-productive.

Classification of Expectorants

According to mechanism of action:

  1. Sedative Expectorant
  2. Stimulant Expectorant

Sedative Expectorants: Stomach irritant, e.g. Bitter drugs like Ipecac, Senega, Indian Squill.

Inorganic Compounds: Antimony potassium tartrate, Ammonium chloride, Sodium citrate, Potassium iodide.

Stimulant Expectorants

Drugs stimulating secretory cells of respiratory tract to increase secretion and dilute sputum.

Example: Eucalyptus, Lemon, Anise.

Ammonium Chloride

Molecular Formula: NH4Cl

Preparation: React hydrochloric acid with ammonia.

NH3 + HCl → NH4Cl

Also prepared by heating ammonium sulphate with sodium chloride:

2NaCl + (NH4)2SO4 → 2NH3 + 2HCl + Na2SO4 → NH4Cl

Properties: White crystalline powder, freely soluble in water, odorless.

Uses of Ammonium Chloride

  • Expectorant in cough preparations.
  • Acidifying agent to correct metabolic alkalosis.
  • Mild antifungal activity.
  • Supplies iodine for thyroid deficiency treatment.

Emetics

  • Drugs that cause forced vomiting to expel stomach contents.
  • Important in poisoning cases.

Mechanism of Action of Emetics

  • Locally acting emetics: Irritating gastric mucosa.
  • Examples: Ammonium bicarbonate, Ipecacuanha.
  • Centrally acting emetics: Act on Chemoreceptor Trigger Zone in brain.
  • Examples: Apomorphine, Morphine.

Uses of Emetics

  • Evacuate toxic substances in stomach (e.g. poisoning).
  • Sometimes used in cough preparations to stimulate respiratory secretions.

Copper Sulphate

Formula: CuSO4.5H2O

Prepared by roasting copper ore or copper in furnace in presence of air.

2Cu + 2H2SO4 + O2 → 2CuSO4 + 2H2O

Properties: Bright blue crystalline granules or powder; dissolves in water; acidic solution.

Haematinics

  • Increase hemoglobin content in blood.
  • Main Haematinics: Iron, Vitamin B12, Folate.

Iron and Dietary Sources

  • Total Iron in body: 2.5-5gm
  • Iron tablets help restore iron levels, best when taken on empty stomach.
  • Sources: Red meat, seafood, beans, dark leafy greens, dried fruits, egg yolk.

Ferrous Gluconate

  • Less gastric irritation than other inorganic ferrous salts.
  • Available as tablets, capsules, elixirs, and intramuscular injections.

Astringents

  • Substance causing contraction/shrinkage of tissues, drying secretions.
  • Applied on skin, mucous membrane without damaging tissue.
  • Examples: Zinc oxide, calamine, alum, zinc chloride, zirconium salts.
  • Improve oily skin by minimizing pores.

Potassium Alum

Formula: KAl(SO4)2

Prepared by adding potassium sulfate to concentrated aluminium sulfate.

K2SO4 + Al2(SO4)3 → 2 KAl(SO4)2

  • Used as an astringent, antiseptic.
  • Topical use for pimples and acne.
  • Used to stop bleeding in hemorrhagic cystitis.

Ferrous Sulphate

Formula: FeSO4.7H2O

Obtained by dissolving iron in excess dilute sulfuric acid.

Fe + H2SO4 → FeSO4 + H2↑

  • Green color crystals, oxidizes to brown ferric salt.
  • Astringent taste, soluble in water, insoluble in alcohol.
  • Decomposes on heating to ferric oxide and sulfur oxides.

Ferrous Gluconate

  • Causes less gastric irritation.
  • Commercially available as tablet, capsule, elixir.
  • Used for intramuscular injection.
  • Has good bioavailability.
